Kate Pendry is back by popular demand!

A five-day workshop allowing a deeper exploration of the process of working with and performing of Shakespeare. With a view to performing a monologue at the Monologbonanza, Det Norske Teatret 21. October.

 

The course is now extended to 5 days to allow for a deeper exploration of the process, and to give each participant a 'solus' hour with the workshop leader  - which the other participants are encouraged to watch.

Introduction to Shakespeare

A workshop suitable for folk with or without experience of performing Shakespeare.

Over five days the workshop will cover:

  • How to read the poetry
  • Discussion around 'newer editions' of the works and whether they were tampered with by - for example - Victorian editors.
  • Shakespeare's 'intentions'.
  • Why Shakespeare isn't funny.
  • Performing Shakespeare, with focus on the hypnotic elements of the language.

 

Workshops participants will work in both the original English, and selected Norwegian translations of: Richard III, Hamlet, King John, Midsummer Night's Dream, Winters Tale.

Preparation for the workshop: Read Hamlet (either in Norwegian or English)

 

You are encouraged to present your monologue at the Monologbonanza at Bikuben, Det Norske Teatret, 21. October!

 

NB! By combining this course with the Creating A Caracter course the previous week,  the participant will be particuarly well equipped to meet  the Shakespeare work with courage and surety. Shakespeare is best approached from the humanist pov - that is deconstructing and contstructing character through an understanding of what makes humans 'tick'.

*It is not mandatory to take the Creating A Character course for participation in the Shakespeare course.

 

 

Kate Pendry (b. 1965, London) is a British actress and playwright based in Oslo. She is known for her character-based one person performances and sometimes controversial themes therein. She has twice been nominated for the national Ibsen Prize, winning the award in 2010. She is currently an associate playwright at Dramatikkens hus, a member of Winter Guests theatre ensemble. A biography of her life and work will be published by Transit forlag in October 2016.
