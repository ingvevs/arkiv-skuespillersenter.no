This is an opportunity for actors to explore their own creative resources by exploring the method of making drama from improvisation developed by the multi-award winning British film and theatre director, Mike Leigh. Using their own first-hand experience and observation, actors create a character, form relationships with other characters and from the ensuing improvisations dramatic situations are developed and explored. This method of creating drama will also be very useful for stage and screen writers.

Paul Clements trained and worked as a schoolteacher in Birmingham before joining the staff of the newly-opened Midlands Arts Centre there as Director of Youth Theatre and Education Services. He was subsequently appointed Fellow in Drama at Manchester University and became founding Artistic Director of Contact Theatre Company. Since then he has taught, acted and directed in the UK, Canada and Scandinavia. He was Principal of Mountview Academy of Theatre Arts in London for twelve years until his retirement in July 2008. Among his publications are The Improvised Play, the first full-length study of the work of the film director, Mike Leigh. In 2002 he was co-winner of The Blue Angel at the Berlin Film Festival for his work on the Danish film, Minor Mishaps, which won the award for Best European Film.

I denne workshopen er det 10 aktive plasser for skuespillere, pluss 4 passive observatørplasser for skuespillere/dramatikere/manusforfattere. Skriv "aktiv" eller "observatør" i adressefeltet i påmeldingen, avhengig av hva slags plass du ønsker.

OBS: Det er noen plasser ledig som aktiv deltaker! (Alle observatørplassene er fullbooket, og det er venteliste. )
