  In this workshop, participants will look at fundamental performance skills, focusing particularly on movement, rhythm and space. We will also look at performers' relationships to one another, and to the audience. The workshop will be highly physical and taught through the group work, games and improvisations Complicite uses in the rehearsal room.

Workshop participants should come dressed in comfortable clothes they can move around in.

Complicite is an international touring theatre company based in London and led by director Simon McBurney. Founded in 1983, Complicite has won over 50 major theatre awards worldwide. Recent work includes A DISAPPEARING NUMBER (winner of the Laurence Olivier Award for Best New Play), SHUN-KIN (created with the Setagaya Public Theatre, Tokyo) and an original opera, A DOG'S HEART. the Company is perhaps best known for its devised work, and for its unusual collaborative devising processes. Although Complicite's productions have taken many different forms over the years, it is also well known for an emphasis on physical and visual forms of storytelling.

The workshop will be lead by Eric Mallett. He trained at LeCoq, and first worked with Complicite on one of the Company's earliest shows, The Phantom Violin. Since then he has been in The Visit, The Street of Crocodiles and Mnemonic. Eric has continued to work closely with Complicite, assisting with the development of A Dog's Heart and a yet-to-be-announced future production.

OBS: The workshop will take place in English!
