   Challenging a young audience - actors, ethics and new writing

  • Can any play be performed for a young audience, or do you have to write plays specifically for each target group?
  • What makes a text suitable for a young audience?
  • How much can we challenge children and teenagers with the plays we perform, and when do we lose them?
  • What are the ethical implications for us as actors and theatre makers when creating work for young audiences?

Through practical work with excerpts from several excellent contemporary European plays, the workshop explores the impact new writing can have on young audiences, debates the responsibility of the actor in relation to the ethics involved when performing for children and teenagers, and aims to challenge the perception of what a play for a young audience can be. Adam Barnard leads the work, sharing from his broad experience working with senstitive issues as a director, playwright and journalist.

The workshop will take place in English.

Final day work sharing
On the last day, the workshop will result in an informal work sharing at Caféteatret / Nordic Black Theatre as part of Theatre Café Festival Oslo, showing selected moments from the texts the group has explored.

It is voluntary to participate in the work sharing.

Theatre Café Festival Oslo
The workshop is organized in collaboration with Oslo International Theatre and is part of Theatre Café Festival Oslo. As a workshop participant you get a free festival pass as a thank you for participating in the work sharing. The festival pass gives you access to all the festival events 25th - 27th april, and includes three meals!

The festival contains rehearsed readings of contemporary European plays for young audiences, guest performances, seminars and debates. Theatre Café Festival Oslo is one of four sister-festivals happening around Europe in 2014 and 2015.

Adam Barnard is a freelance theatre director, playwright and journalist. He was Joint Director of Company of Angels from 2011-2013, one of the leading theatre companies for yong audiences in Great Britain. The awardwinning, Arts Council-funded company makes bold and experimental new work for, with and about young people, in theatre and other media.

Directing credits include Nordost (UK tour), Korttegnerens song (Copenhagen), and Look Back In Anger & Midsummer Night’s Dream (Lichfield Garrick Theatre). Writing credits include Disconnected (European tour), How I Came To Be (Theatre 503, London) and A Much Much Much Better World (RADA, London). As a journalist, Adam has done investigative journalism for both The Times and the Guardian.
