The Actors' Center is proud to present Kate Pendry in our Theatre Writing Series, where we engage actors and performers with a strong pen to share their personal artistic method of writing.

WORKSHOP 2: Creating a character

The workshop focuses on creating a character and storyline with a view to performing a monologue - or one-person theatre piece.

 

 Over three days the workshop will cover:

  • Creating a character based on a real person: the pros and cons, including whether the artist has a responsibility to the source person.
  • Creating a fictional character - including 're-imagining' classic characters in the public domain (e.g. Hamlet, Hedda, Faust)
  • Choosing a story/situation: why the character is telling this story, to whom, fourth wall or not, dramaturgy and 'message' - if any.

The last day of the workshop ends with presentations of 5 minute 'start' monologues, created by the participants.

Bio:

Kate Pendry (b. 1965, London) is a British actress and playwright based in Oslo. She is known for her character-based one person performances and sometimes controversial themes therein. She has twice been nominated for the national Ibsen Prize, winning the award in 2010.

She is currently playwright-in-residence at Dramatikkens hus, a member of Winter Guests theatre ensemble, and hosts the Sister Ruby Tuesday Soul Show on RadiOrakel.
