   I samarbeid med Det Andre Teatret inviterer vi Edinburgh-hiten Trygve Wakenshaw til Skuespillersenteret for å holde kurs i slapstick og fysisk komedie!

Workshopen holdes på engelsk.

En ny retning innen komedie
Wakenshaw representerer en ny retning innen visuell komedie som er i ferd med å bre om seg. Forestillingen Squidboy, som gjester Det Andre Teatret i januar, har blitt overøst med stjerner i både australsk og skotsk presse.

Edinburghs Fest Magazine skriver:
"In Wakenshaw’s masterful hands the audience completely buys into the physical and narrative laws of his world. He has atomic clock timing. He knows the joy and the power of repetition. And it’s significantly funnier than the majority of the Fringe’s comedy programme."

About Squidboy
A stream-of-conciousness physical comedy.

WINNER: Auckland Fringe Award for Best Performance, 2013
RUNNER UP: Auckland Fringe Award for Best Production, 2013
NOMINATED: Total Theatre Award, Edinburgh Festival Fringe, 2013
NOMINATED: Brighton Fringe Emerging Talent Award, 2013

Gaulier-trained Trygve Wakenshaw delights in this award-winning, critically acclaimed physical comedy about an imaginary friend who makes imaginary friends who make imaginary enemies who make imaginary armies out of imaginary Scotsmen. What will happen? Nobody knows. Not even the squid.

About Trygve Wakenshaw
Trygve Wakenshaw is a New Zealand born actor and comedian, now based in London.
He studied for two years in Paris at Ecole Philippe Gaulier, the prestigious school with alumni including Sasha Baron-Cohen, Emma Thompson and founding members of Complicite and Spymonkey. Trygve has taught around the world the arts of physical theatre, mime and slapstick.
