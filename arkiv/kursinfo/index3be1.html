    Michael Chekhov Workshop with Jeffrey Glickman

Study The Michael Chekhov Technique instructed by American actor and experienced Michael Chekhov technique instructor, Jeffrey Glickman. Jeffrey Glickman says that The Actors Gym Workshop is for both new and veteran actors to come and work together, blast through boundaries and take risks to become a more fine tuned, specific and COMPELLING ACTOR – not just a competent one.

Actors will work with the following principles and practical applications:

INSTRUMENT - INSPIRATION - EMOTION

Having instructed hundreds of actors/directors/performers on the Michael Chekhov technique, Glickman has designed a clear and effective process; one that provides specific tools performers need to live spontaneously and DO WHAT THEY REALLY WANT TO DO; TAKE ACTIONS and SURPRISE THEMSELVES. Glickman’s ultimate goal is to “free yourself, from yourself”.

 

Preparation - IMPORTANT!
Jeffrey can fascilitate both actors wishing to work in English and in Norwegian. Those participants wishing to work with English text will be sent short monologue pieces taken from Edgar Lee Master's Spoon River Anthology - very poetic works.

Those who wish to work in Norwegian will have to send a copy of their chosen text in advance to post(a)skuespillersenter.no

Write what language you choose in the field marked "Annet" when you sign up!

About Jeffrey Glickman
Jeffrey Glickman is a graduate of New York University, Tisch School of the Arts. He studied at the Michael Chekhov Studio, NYC/Los Angeles directly with acclaimed actresses Beatrice Straight, Mala Powers in addition to Ted Pugh and Fern Sloan. Glickman has also studied and learned from many of the best - The Circle-In-The-Square Conservatory (New York), The William Esper Studio (New York), The Actors Space (New York), The Lee Strasberg Studio (Los Angeles), The Darryl Hickman Studio (Los Angeles) and with Tony Greco (Los Angeles) to finally return to the Michael Chekhov Studio West and begin instructing.

His goal of sharing the unique Chekhov tools with actors on an international level began in Milano and Paris beginning in 1990. Since then Glickman has been an international actor and instructor with over two decades of experience training actors. Glickman founded The Actors Gym Studio (TAG) and has taught extensively throughout the US and Europe. He now resides in Stockholm and is the only instructor in Sweden currently working diligently to bring the secrets of the Chekhov technique work to artists throughout Scandinavia.
