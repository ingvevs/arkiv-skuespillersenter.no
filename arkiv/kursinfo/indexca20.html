If it wasn’t for free jazz.

A workshop for you with Mårten Spångberg.

Presence, spectatorship, emancipation, participation, socially engaged, documentary, accelerationism, fake this and that, therapy, practice based, identity politics, queer-theatre, post-internet, indigo, proceduralism are all great but then what? What’s wrong with lables, brands, social networks, selfies, tweet performance but then what? Even more immaterial or the return to Fordism, a cry for authenticity, more theory or perhaps intuition, sharing, exchange, commons and visual art, but then what? No to freelance, free, independent, to institutions too, but then what?

Erase. Let’s start from the beginning, somewhere else. The beginning really, let’s just show up for the very first instance, no preparation, expectation, precision, quality, assumption. No deals, no liberty and no no liberty. Let dance, theatre, performance, ritual come together and become the world, earth and the planet at once. Let’s start without responsibility, and without play, without politics and without aesthetics.

 

Performance, horizon, intensity

With a starting point in his last two performance La Substance and The Internet Mårten Spångberg will guide the group through both a practical and theoretical landscape in order to examine some questions about contemporary performance and it’s relation to practice, politics, policy and planning.

The workshop will attempt to detect, discuss and dissect some issues that performing arts today are facing in regard of relations to aesthetics, engagement and responsibility.

How can performing arts create it’s own forms of sensitivity, autonomous experiences that does not get trapped in criticism, interpretation and self-referentiality.

 

Mårten Spångberg

Mårten Spångberg is a choreographer living and working in Stockholm. His interests concern choreography in an expanded field, something that he has approached through experimental practices and creative process in multiplicity of formats and expressions. He has been active on stage as performer and creator since '94, and has since '99 created his own choreographies, from solos to larger scale works, which has toured internationally. Under the label International Festival he collaborated with the architect Tor Lindstrand he engaged in social and expanded choreography. From 1996 – 2005 Spångberg organized and curated festivals in Sweden and internationally.He initiated the network organization INPEX in 2006, has thorough experience in teaching both theory and practice and was director for the MA program in choreography at the Univ. of Dance in Stockholm 2008 - 2012. In 2011 his first book Spangbergianism was published..
