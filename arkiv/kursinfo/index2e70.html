SCREEN ACTING  - A Basic Introduction

A two-day workshop for actors who want a basic introduction to Screen Acting for Film. Led by award-winning Irish Film Director Yvonne McDevitt, this workshop will give you a basic, and applied introduction to Acting for Film, using cutting-edge digital film equipment and technology:

 

  • How to build good creative relationships with fellow Actors, Director and Crew
  • How to understand exactly what is required of you as an actor on a film set
  • How to prepare yourself for camera
  • How to improvise for camera
  • How to work with other Actors on camera
  • How to maximise your potential on film
  • How to approach getting Film acting work in Norway, Europe, and internationally

 

Yvonne McDevitt read Drama at Trinity College Dublin. Thereafter she was a Dramaturg for Robert Wilson at L'Opéra Bastille in Paris. After an apprenticeship at the Royal Court, Young Vic and National Theatres in London, her productions played on the main-stages of Russia (Hermitage Theatre, Moscow), France (Théâtre National Populaire, Lyon) and Norway (National Theatre, Oslo). She was awarded a JERWOOD DIRECTORS AWARD at the Young Vic theatre in London and the JUDITH E. WILSON Drama Fellowship at the University of Cambridge. Her first feature film – 2GRAVES – with music by Oscar-nominated composer Michael Nyman – premiered at the East End Film Festival in London in 2013. She was selected for BERLINALE TALENTS at the 64th Berlin International Film Festival in 2014. HORSE – her first short film, edited by French Editor, Nelly Quettier (César-nominated for – HOLY MOTORS – by Leos Carax, 2012), premiered at the Galway Film Fleadh in 2014. TIME – her second short film premiered at the Cannes Film Festival 2015.
