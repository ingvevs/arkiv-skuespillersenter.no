  Nora Armani kommer til Bergen for å tilby en todagers Audition-workshop.

Nora Armani har lang erfaring som skuespiller for film og teater. Hun er også film- og teaterinstruktør og filmprodusent. Hun har bodd vekselvis i London og Paris, men er nå bosatt i New York. Her har hun tilegnet seg førstehåndserfaring fra auditions og hvordan gjøre en god presentasjon av seg selv.

Kurset vil foregå på engelsk. Workshopen er et samarbeid mellom Bergen Byspill og Norsk Skuespillersenter.

Armani skriver selv om workshopen:

The Audition Workshop
-Presenting yourself under the best light -

The Audition Workshop I propose is a two-day workshop for actors to fine-tune their instrument through simple exercises that free them of all useless tensions that prevent showcasing their talent and craft under the best light.

During these two days what casting directors or accepting committees look for is discussed in-depth. Reinforcing confidence through re-evaluating one’s purpose and career choices, choice of material, choice of look and clothing are evaluated and discussed. Optimizing chances using physical and emotional range are explored for each participant. Further examples are brought from various audition situations and countries: UK, France & USA.

Day One starts with a group jamming session to warm up the voice and get familiar with the proposed audition materials. Exercises that help create a state of relaxation are conducted as a group. Then texts are broken down into their emotional and thought contents. Transitions are identified and marked. Individual feedback and pointers are given to help improve the audition process and the actual presentation. Practical suggestions are made by the workshop conductor to each participant individually to help improve their audition process and create the optimum conditions for being seen.

Day Two is used as if it were a “Call Back” session. The first day’s work is re-evaluated through feedback on the adjustments made by the actors and how the proposed suggestions from Day One are incorporated into the work. The participants are allowed to ‘role play’ the full audition process as though they were in a real audition situation. This is followed by discussion and feedback.

Areas covered during the two days include:
a)     Feedback on choice of material and its suitability. PRODUCT.

b)    The day before, the night before and the moment before entering the audition room: PREPARATION.

c)     Best behaviour in the audition room. Incorporating space, time and the conditions: PRESENCE.

d)    Freshness of thought and clarity of emotion. Speaking lines as though for the first time: PERFORMANCE.
