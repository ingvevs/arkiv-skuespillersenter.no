Introduction to Shakespeare

A two-day workshop suitable for folk with or without experience of performing Shakespeare.

Over two days the workshop will cover:

  • How to read the poetry
  • Discussion around 'newer editions' of the works and whether they were tampered with by - for example - Victorian editors.
  • Shakespeare's 'intentions'.
  • Why Shakespeare isn't funny.
  • Performing Shakespeare, with focus on the hypnotic elements of the language.

 Workshops participants will work in both the original English, and selected Norwegian translations of: Richard iii, Hamlet, King John, Midsummer Night's Dream, Winters Tale.

Bio:

Kate Pendry (b. 1965, London) is a British actress and playwright based in Oslo. She is known for her character-based one person performances and sometimes controversial themes therein. She has twice been nominated for the national Ibsen Prize, winning the award in 2010.

She is currently playwright-in-residence at Dramatikkens hus, a member of Winter Guests theatre ensemble, and has hosts the Sister Ruby Tuesday Soul Show on RadiOrakel.
