Creating a character

The workshop focuses on creating a character and storyline with a view to performing a monologue - or one-person theatre piece.

The course is now extended to 5 days to allow for a deeper exploration of the process, and to give each participant a 'solus' hour with the workshop leader  - which the other participants are encouraged to watch.

 

Over five days the workshop will cover:

  • Creating a character based on a real person: the pros and cons, including whether the artist has a responsibility to the source person.
  • Creating a fictional character - including 're-imagining' classic characters in the public domain (e.g. Hamlet, Hedda, Faust)
  • Choosing a story/situation: why the character is telling this story, to whom, fourth wall or not, dramaturgy and 'message' - if any.

 

The last day of the workshop ends with presentations of 5 minute 'start' monologues, created by the participants.

The workshop will be held mainly in English, but the participants can write in Norwegian.

 

You are also encouraged to present your monologue at the Monologbonanza at Bikuben, Det Norske Teatret, 21. October!

 

NB! By combining this course with the Shakespeare course the following week,  the participant will be particularly well equipped to meet the Shakespeare work with courage and surety. Shakespeare is best approached from the humanist point of view - that is deconstructing and constructing character through an understanding of what makes humans 'tick'.

*It is not mandatory to take the Creating A Character course for participation in the Shakespeare course.

 

Kate Pendry (b. 1965, London) is a British actress and playwright based in Oslo. She is known for her character-based one person performances and sometimes controversial themes therein. She has twice been nominated for the national Ibsen Prize, winning the award in 2010. She is currently an associate playwright at Dramatikkens hus, a member of Winter Guests theatre ensemble. A biography of her life and work will be published by Transit forlag in October 2016.
