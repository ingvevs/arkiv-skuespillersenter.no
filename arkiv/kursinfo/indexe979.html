About actor-musicianship
Over the last decade actor/musician work, in which the actors provide the orchestra as well as the singing, acting and dancing, has become a popular form of theatre. One famous example is the revival of Sweeney Todd which ran for six months in the West End before opening on Broadway in 2005, winning two prestigious Tony Awards.

In the UK actor-musicianship has become a career in its own right, so much that there is even a well-known specialised Actor Musician Degree course at Rose Bruford Drama College in London. Head tutor and esteemed actor-musician Jeremy Harrison is coming to Norsk Skuespillersenter leading an introductory workshop for actors who play instruments!

Jeremy Harrison was a freelance theatre practitioner for 13 years before joining the staff of Rose Bruford College. He was a core member of John Doyle’s award winning company of actor musicians from 1995 to 2001. He has appeared in the West End and in numerous seasons in regional theatres including: West Yorkshire Playhouse, York Theatre Royal andNew Wolsey Theatre Ipswich. His film and television credits include ITV's Londons Burning and The Bill and two series of Playing the Field for the BBC.
Jeremy has written music and been musical director for a range of work in film and theatre including Caterpillar which won the RTA Award for Best Fiction in 2010.
Jeremy is Programme Director of the Actor Musicianship Programme at Rose Bruford College.
